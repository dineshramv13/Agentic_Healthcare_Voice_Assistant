# Appointment Policy

## Booking an Appointment

Patients can book a GP appointment in three ways:

1. **Online booking** via the patient portal (available 24/7). Log in with your NHS number and date of birth, then select "Book Appointment". Online booking is open for routine appointments up to 3 weeks in advance.
2. **By phone**, calling the surgery between 8:00 AM and 6:30 PM, Monday to Friday. Phone lines are busiest between 8:00 AM and 9:30 AM — patients are encouraged to call after 10:00 AM for non-urgent matters.
3. **In person** at the reception desk during opening hours.

## Same-Day Appointments

A limited number of same-day "urgent" slots are released every morning at 8:00 AM on a first-come, first-served basis. These are intended for problems that cannot wait but are not emergencies (e.g. a worsening infection, sudden pain, a child with a high fever). Same-day slots cannot be booked online — patients must call the surgery directly.

## Appointment Types and Durations

- **Routine GP appointment**: 10 minutes
- **Nurse appointment** (dressings, blood tests, vaccinations): 15 minutes
- **Extended consultation** (multiple issues, mental health review, new diagnosis discussion): 20 minutes — must be requested explicitly when booking
- **Telephone consultation**: 10 minutes, available for follow-ups and medication reviews that don't require a physical examination

## Cancellations and Rescheduling

Patients should cancel or reschedule at least 2 hours before their appointment time wherever possible. Cancellations can be made online, by phone, or by replying to the SMS reminder with "CANCEL". Repeated no-shows (3 or more missed appointments without cancellation in a 12-month period) may result in a review of the patient's booking privileges.

## Booking on Behalf of Someone Else

Appointments can be booked on behalf of a child, elderly relative, or dependent adult if you are listed as their registered carer or next of kin on file. For adults not listed as a dependent, the patient must either book themselves or provide verbal consent during a recorded phone call.

## New Patient Registration

New patients must register with the surgery before their first appointment can be booked. Registration requires proof of address (a utility bill or bank statement dated within the last 3 months) and photo ID. Registration can be started online but must be finalised in person within 14 days.
