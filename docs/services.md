# Services Available

## Core Services

- General GP consultations (in person and telephone)
- Nurse-led clinics: vaccinations, dressings, ear syringing, travel health advice
- Phlebotomy (blood test) clinic, weekday mornings 8:00 AM – 11:00 AM
- Chronic disease management clinics: diabetes, asthma, COPD, hypertension — typically reviewed every 3 to 6 months depending on condition stability
- Maternity and antenatal shared care with the local hospital
- Childhood immunisation programme, following the NHS routine vaccination schedule
- Contraception services, including long-acting reversible contraception (LARC) fittings by appointment with Dr. Thompson
- Minor surgery for minor skin lesions, with Dr. Chen
- Mental health support, including initial assessment and referral to talking therapies (IAPT) or community mental health teams where appropriate

## Referrals

GPs can refer patients to hospital specialists, community services, or allied health professionals (physiotherapy, podiatry, dietetics) where clinically appropriate. Referral letters are typically sent within 5 working days of the consultation. Patients can check referral status via the patient portal under "My Referrals," though processing times for the receiving hospital or service are outside the surgery's control.

## Test Results

Blood test and other diagnostic results are typically available within 3 to 5 working days, viewable on the patient portal once reviewed by a GP. Patients should not assume "no news is good news" — if a result is not visible after 7 working days, they should contact the surgery directly. Urgent or abnormal results that require immediate action will be communicated proactively by phone, not left for the patient to discover on the portal.

## Health Checks

Patients aged 40 to 74 without an existing cardiovascular condition are eligible for a free NHS Health Check every 5 years, covering blood pressure, cholesterol, BMI, and lifestyle risk factors. These can be booked as a standalone nurse appointment.

## Sick Notes (Fit Notes)

For absences of 7 days or fewer, employees can usually self-certify with their employer and do not need a GP fit note. For absences longer than 7 days, a fit note can be requested via the patient portal or at a consultation; the first fit note for a new condition typically requires a consultation, but follow-up fit notes for the same ongoing condition can sometimes be issued without a further appointment, at GP discretion.

## Travel Health

Travel vaccination and malaria advice clinics run by appointment with the practice nurse. Patients should book at least 6 to 8 weeks before travel, as some vaccines require multiple doses spaced weeks apart. Some travel vaccines are NHS-funded; others (e.g. certain malaria medications, some non-routine travel vaccines) are private and charged at the surgery's private fee schedule.
